# Memory: Ronald Andrade

## Personal Profile

Ronald Andrade is a retired CNC machinist, tool-and-die specialist, and lifelong union organiser born in East San Jose to a Portuguese-American family whose grandparents emigrated from São Miguel, Azores, in the 1920s. He has lived in Alum Rock his entire life, raised two children in the Cape Cod he and Maria bought in 1981, and identifies as a Portuguese-American, working-class, Catholic, US Army veteran in roughly that order. His worldview was shaped by decades on the Bayshore Industries shop floor, the long stretch as a union steward and chief organiser for United Machinists and Toolworkers Local 1527, and the Vietnam-era Army service that gave him his VFW post. He is plain, blunt, principled, dryly funny, and privately sentimental; he prefers people who show up on time, speak straight, and keep their word. He has been a widower since December 2024, when Maria died of COPD complications after 48 years of marriage, and that loss continues to shape the texture of his days more than he says.

## Work & Projects

Ronald retired from Bayshore Industries in 2017 after 42 years on the floor: apprentice 1975, journeyman 1980, senior machinist 1990, union steward 1990, chief steward and lead organiser 2002 to 2017. He led the 2008 and 2014 contract negotiations and counts those as his proudest professional achievements. He draws a $2,800-per-month Bayshore pension and still speaks two to three times a year at the Pacific Coast Labor Center on grievance procedure and contract strategy; a guest lecture on the 2008 contract fight is on the schedule for later in 2026 and the draft was started in February 2026. He has been active in VFW Post 3982 since 1975, served as post commander 2005 to 2008, organises the annual Veterans Day ceremony and Memorial Day cookout, and drives fellow veterans to VA appointments through the post's assistance program. He sits on monthly meetings of the Alum Rock Neighborhood Association and helps coordinate the Holy Ghost Festival each year at Five Wounds Portuguese National Church.

Ronald lives with mild-to-moderate COPD diagnosed in 2019 from machine-shop dust exposure; he takes Spiriva (tiotropium) daily and uses a Ventolin rescue inhaler as needed; his last pulmonary function test in December 2025 showed FEV1 at 68% of predicted, stable; extreme cold triggers shortness of breath. He quit smoking in 1998 after 25 years and is current on flu, pneumonia, and COVID vaccinations. He had a right-knee replacement in September 2025 at Stonebridge Healthcare; physical therapy completed January 2026; mornings remain stiff and he uses a cane on bad days; the one-year follow-up with Dr. Angela Petersen is Thursday, October 15, 2026. Hypertension is well-controlled on Losartan 50mg daily with blood pressure running 130 to 138 over 78 to 84. Dental care runs through Dr. Frank Oliveira; the next cleaning is Thursday, October 29, 2026. Vision is corrected with bifocals updated in January 2026. He is grieving Maria; he is not in therapy; sleep is poor with frequent wakings at 3 to 4 AM and evenings are the hardest part of the day. Exercise consists of walking 20 to 30 minutes most days, Tuesday bowling, and weekend fishing.

## Home & Living

The Andrade house is a three-bedroom Cape Cod in Alum Rock, San Jose, built 1948 and bought in 1981, now paid off. The main floor holds the living room, kitchen, dining room, a downstairs bedroom Ronald moved into after the knee surgery, and a bathroom. Upstairs are two bedrooms: the grandchildren's room and Maria's sewing room, kept untouched. The basement holds the workbench, hand tools, and woodworking shop; the garage is split between the wood shop and the truck. The backyard has a vegetable garden, a grill, and a flagpole that Ronald raises and lowers daily. The front porch has two chairs that Ronald uses in the evenings. Open house items: gutter cleaning, furnace inspection before winter, and grab-bar installation in the downstairs bathroom (Carla pushing, Ronald still resisting).
